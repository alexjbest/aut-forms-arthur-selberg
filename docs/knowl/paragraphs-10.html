Digression: bounds on fourier coefficients of cusp forms
Theorem1.49Hecke

If \(f(z)\) is a cusp form of weight \(k\)

\begin{equation*} f(z) = \sum_{n=1}^\infty a_n e(nz) \end{equation*}

then

\begin{equation*} a_n = O( n^{k/2}) \end{equation*}

called the Hecke or trivial bound.

If \(\lambda_n = n^{(1-k)/2} a_n\) then this says \(\lambda_n \le \sqrt n\text{.}\)
